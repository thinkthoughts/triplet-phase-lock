# Glossary

## Core Paths

**IA** — invalid assignment (ZOS path)  
- sequence of unconstrained or inconsistent mappings  
- produces domain mismatch or collapse  

**VC** — valid construction (GOS path)  
- sequence of constraint-preserving relations  
- maintains domain consistency and structure  

---

## Origin Framework

**ZOS** — Zero-Origin Story  
- reasoning that collapses to or begins from zero  
- permits unconstrained assignment and inconsistency  
- corresponds to IA  

**GOS** — Gravitational-Origin Structure  
- reasoning anchored by constraint-preserving relations  
- resists collapse and accumulates structure across stages  
- corresponds to VC  

*(“gravitational” denotes stability and resistance to collapse, not a specific physical model)*

---

## ZABCD (failure classification)

- **Z0** — unconstrained  
- **Z1** — collapse  
- **Z2** — assignment  
- **Z3** — denial  
- **Z4** — mixed inconsistency  

Interpretation:
- progression of failure through loss of constraint and domain mismatch  

---

## ABCD (valid construction)

- **A** — anchor  
- **B** — bilateral  
- **C** — constraint  
- **D** — domain consistency  

Interpretation:
- minimal components required for stable, valid construction  

---

## Triplet

\[
\Pi^{(n)} = (Pi_i, Pi_{i+1}, Pi_{i+2})
\]

- staged unit of construction or assignment  
- shared structure across IA and VC paths  

---

## Progression

- **Π⁽⁰⁾ — expand**  
- **Π⁽¹⁾ — extend**  
- **Π⁽²⁾ — resist**  
- **Π⁽³⁾ — synthesis**  

Interpretation:
- IA accumulates inconsistency across stages  
- VC accumulates structure across stages  

---

## Key Relation

\[
\text{construction} \neq \text{assignment}
\]

- distinguishes constraint-preserving structure from arbitrary mapping  

---

## Summary

- ZOS → IA → inconsistency  
- GOS → VC → structure
